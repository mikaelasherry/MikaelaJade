The origin

This silver is
older than
the Earth.

Around five billion years ago, a star many times the size of our sun reached the end of its life. In a single pulse of catastrophic light — a supernova — it forged every heavy element that didn't exist before: iron, gold, silver, the atoms that make up gemstones. Then it scattered them across space.

Our solar system formed from that debris. The silver settled deep in the Earth's crust. The gemstones crystallized over millions of years under heat and pressure. Eventually, they made their way into careful hands — and into the piece you're considering wearing.

You are made of the same elements. The carbon in your body, the iron in your blood — all of it from the same supernovae. When you wear a piece of Mikaela Jade jewelry, you are a piece of the universe wearing itself.

Materials & provenance

Every claim
has a receipt.

We don't say "sustainably sourced" and leave it there. Every material in every Mikaela Jade piece has a named origin, a certified supply chain, and a story we can tell in full.

Recycled 925 sterling silver

SCS-certified recycled sterling. Refined by Hoover & Strong from recovered electronics and industrial sources. Chemically identical to mined silver. Audited annually.

Semi-precious gemstones

Sourced from named mines and trusted small-scale suppliers. Moonstone from Sri Lanka. Malachite from the DRC. Amethyst from Brazil. Every stone has a provenance document.

Production

Small-batch. Made by hand in a certified workshop. Fair wages verified. Each piece is made to order — we don't hold excess inventory or produce to landfill.

Packaging

Recycled kraft box. Linen wrap. Seed paper intention card — plant it when you're done. Soy ink. Zero plastic. The box is part of the ritual, not waste.
